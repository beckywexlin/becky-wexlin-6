## The Competitive Landscape

The weird/funny graphic tee market breaks into three tiers:

1. **Marketplaces** (Threadless, Redbubble, TeePublic, Etsy) — massive catalogs, millions of monthly visitors, domain authority 80+. You can't out-SEO them on volume. But their content is thin (auto-generated product pages) and they have zero brand personality.

2. **Mid-tier indie brands** (Wicked Clothes, Threadheads, Solid Threads, Cool Shirtz, Strange Ways) — established brands with real followings, review counts in the thousands, and niche positioning. Domain authority ~30–60. This is your competitive tier.

3. **Your tier** (Becky Wexlin, Shirtmandude, smaller Etsy sellers) — new/small independent brands with strong design but near-zero organic visibility. Domain authority <20.

## What Your Competitors Do That You Don't (Yet)

### 1. Reviews — The #1 gap
Wicked Clothes has 7,386 reviews. Sourpuss claims 20,000+. You have zero visible reviews anywhere on your site. **This is the single biggest trust gap.** Reviews feed Product schema (stars in Google results), reduce purchase anxiety, and generate unique content on every product page.

**Action**: Add a review system (Judge.me or Loox — both integrate with static sites via JS widget). Send a post-purchase email via Klaviyo asking for reviews. Even 50 reviews across your catalog would transform your credibility.

### 2. Product Naming for Search Intent
Your products are named for creativity ("T-Rexcellent," "Harambe Warhol," "Rule #62"). Your competitors name products for what people search: "Funny Dinosaur T-Shirt," "Dark Humor Cat Tee," "Retro Skull Graphic T-Shirt."

**Action**: Keep your creative names as display names, but restructure your `<title>` tags and meta descriptions to lead with searchable phrases:
- Current: `T-Rexcellent T-Shirt — Becky Wexlin Creative`
- Better: `Funny Dinosaur Graphic T-Shirt — T-Rexcellent | Becky Wexlin`

### 3. Collection/Category Pages
Threadless, Wicked Clothes, and Threadheads all have well-structured collection pages (`/collections/funny-t-shirts`, `/collections/animal-tees`, etc.) that rank for category-level searches.

You have `/shop.html` (one page for everything) and `/collections/graphic-tees.html` (one collection). You're missing:
- `/collections/funny-t-shirts`
- `/collections/skull-t-shirts`
- `/collections/animal-t-shirts`
- `/collections/santa-barbara-tees`
- `/collections/retro-graphic-tees`

**Action**: Create 4–5 themed collection pages with unique H1s, intros, and filtered product grids. Each one targets a different search cluster.

### 4. Bundle/Multi-Buy Pricing
Wicked Clothes drives AOV with "Buy 2 save 10%, Buy 6+ save 50%." You have no bundle incentive.

**Action**: Consider a "Buy 2, Get 10% Off" or "3 for $50" offer. Even if margins are tight, the AOV lift usually pays for itself.

### 5. Email Incentive Strength
Sourpuss offers 10% off + WELCOME20 code. You have a popup but your incentive positioning could be stronger on the homepage.

### 6. Cause/Mission Messaging
Wicked Clothes prominently displays "$150K+ donated to important causes." The Mountain ties to sustainability and ocean conservation. You have a strong brand voice but no visible cause or mission statement beyond the product.
