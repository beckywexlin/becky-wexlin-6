Keep it looking good

Care guide.

How to wash, dry, and store your Becky Wexlin shirt so the print stays sharp and the fabric holds up. Specific instructions for both the Gildan 5000 and Bella+Canvas 3001.

For all shirts

Universal care rules

These rules apply to every shirt in the Becky Wexlin collection regardless of which blank it's printed on.

1

Turn inside out before washing

This is the single most impactful thing you can do. Turning the shirt inside out means the print faces away from the washing machine drum, other garments, and the agitation that causes surface friction. That friction — even at low temperatures — is one of the primary causes of print degradation. Make it automatic.

2

Wash in cold water

Use cold water at 30°C / 86°F or below. Heat breaks down the bond between ink and fabric — both screen print and DTG inks are vulnerable to hot water over time. Cold water cleans effectively and dramatically extends the life of the print. Use a gentle or delicate cycle to reduce agitation further.

3

Use a gentle detergent — skip the fabric softener

Choose a gentle or non-biological detergent. Biological detergents contain enzymes that are effective at stain removal but harder on print inks over time. Avoid fabric softener entirely — it leaves a residue on fabric fibres that dulls print colours and can interfere particularly with DTG prints.

4

Air dry whenever possible

Heat is the enemy of printed fabric. Tumble dryers run at temperatures that damage print quality over time and add mechanical friction on top. Air drying is by far the best option. Hang on a clothes horse away from direct heat and sunlight, or lay flat. If you must use a dryer, use the lowest heat setting and remove the shirt while still slightly damp to finish air drying.

5

Never iron directly on the print

Direct heat from an iron will damage the print. If ironing is necessary, turn inside out and iron on a low setting, keeping the iron away from any areas where the print shows through. Better yet, air dry flat and most shirts come out wrinkle-free without needing to be ironed at all.

6

Store correctly between wears

Folding is better than hanging for long-term storage. Fold print-side in so the design isn't rubbing against other items in the drawer. Store in a dry location — humidity between wears is a slow version of the same damage that washing machines do. Avoid storing over radiators or in direct sunlight.

Don't

  • Tumble dry on high heat

Gildan 5000 shrinkage note: The Gildan 5000 is made from pre-shrunk cotton, which significantly reduces shrinkage compared to untreated cotton. You should see minimal shrinkage when washed in cold water. Washing in warm or hot water, or tumble drying on high heat, can cause noticeable shrinkage in any cotton garment. Stick to cold wash and air dry for a consistent fit over time.

The lighter 145 GSM weight means it dries quickly on a clothes horse, usually within a couple of hours.

Accidents happen

Stain removal tips

Act fast

Treat stains as quickly as possible — the longer a stain sits, the harder it is to remove. Blot (don't rub) excess liquid first.

Treat from the back

Apply stain remover to the back of the fabric, pushing the stain back out the way it came in rather than through the print.

Use a gentle stain remover

Choose a gentle stain remover or a small amount of washing-up liquid. Avoid bleach-based products entirely — they will damage both the fabric and the print.

Wash normally after treating

Once treated, wash according to the care instructions above — cold water, inside out, gentle cycle.

Don't put a stained shirt in the dryer

Heat sets stains permanently. Always check the stain has gone before putting a shirt anywhere near heat.
