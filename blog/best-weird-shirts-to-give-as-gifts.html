Gift guide

The best weird shirts to give as gifts (for people who hate normal gifts)

February 10, 2025·5 min read

There is a specific person in everyone's life who is genuinely difficult to buy for — not because they don't have preferences, but because their preferences are specific, strange, and completely resistant to the usual gift categories. A candle does not speak to them. A gift card feels like a forfeit. What they actually want is something that makes them say "this is exactly me" the moment they open it.

Why a graphic tee is actually a great gift

Clothing as a gift has a bad reputation, largely because most people play it safe. A plain shirt in a neutral colour from a brand the recipient doesn't care about is not a gift — it's a placeholder. But a graphic tee chosen with actual knowledge of the person? That's one of the most personal gifts you can give.

A shirt says: I know what you find funny. I know what references you get. I know your sense of humour is a little off-centre and I respect that about you. That's a harder thing to communicate with most other gifts.

There's also a practical dimension. People wear their favourite shirts constantly — far more than they use most gift items — which means the right graphic tee has a long lifespan as a reminder that someone understood them.

Who is this gift for?

The weird shirt gift works best for a specific personality type. They tend to:

  • Have a developed and idiosyncratic sense of humour
  • Spend a meaningful amount of time online and understand meme culture
  • Wear their personality through their clothing already
  • Have strong opinions about things most people don't think about
  • Be the person in a group who finds things funny that others don't immediately get

If this describes someone you're buying for, a well-chosen weird shirt will land harder than almost anything else in the same price range.

What makes a weird shirt actually giftable

Not all weird shirts are good gifts. There's a spectrum, and the gifts that work best sit in a specific part of it.

Too obscure is a real problem. If the reference requires explanation, the shirt loses most of its power. The best graphic tee gifts are ones that the recipient understands immediately — the laugh of recognition is the whole point.

Too aggressive can misfire. A shirt that's genuinely offensive or politically charged might be funny between close friends but can read very differently depending on the occasion and the relationship.

Too cheap undermines everything. A shirt with a great design printed on thin, low-quality fabric communicates that the idea was there but the follow-through wasn't. Quality matters. The shirt should feel like something, not like a disposable novelty item.

The sweet spot is a shirt with a design that's genuinely clever, made on fabric that's genuinely good, at a price point that says you thought about it without overclaiming.

How to choose the right design for someone else

Buying a graphic tee for someone else is a test of how well you actually know them. A few useful questions to ask yourself:

  • What do they reference constantly in conversation? Inside jokes, specific media, particular interests?
  • What kind of humour makes them genuinely laugh — absurdist, dry, surreal, self-deprecating?
  • What's their relationship with irony? Some people love a shirt that's funny because it's earnest; others prefer one that's funny because it's deliberately strange.
  • What do they already wear? If their wardrobe is mostly dark colours, a subtle print on black or charcoal will get worn. If they lean bright, go bolder.

The closer you can get to something that feels like it was made specifically for them, the better the gift lands.

A note on sizing for graphic tee gifts

Sizing is the historic obstacle in clothing gifts, but it's less fraught with graphic tees than with most other clothing. Graphic tees are generally worn with a relaxed fit, which means there's more tolerance for being slightly off in either direction.

The best approach: if you know their size, order it. If you genuinely don't know, lean one size larger rather than smaller — a slightly oversized tee reads as intentional, whereas a too-small tee just doesn't get worn. Most size guides for graphic tees include chest and length measurements that make it easier to estimate.

The right weird shirt tells someone: I see you. I know exactly what kind of strange you are. I approve.
