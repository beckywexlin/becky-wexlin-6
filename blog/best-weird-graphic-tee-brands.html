The best weird graphic tee brands in 2025 (ranked by someone who refuses to be normal)

May 2025·12 min read

There are a lot of graphic tee brands out there. Most of them are boring. Some are trying to be weird and failing. A small handful are genuinely, wonderfully strange — and those are the ones worth your money.

We tested, wore, and obsessively compared the best weird graphic tee brands of 2025 so you don’t have to scroll through thousands of mediocre options on your own. This list covers original art, unusual concepts, print quality that holds up after 40 washes, and — most importantly — the ability to make a stranger on the street do a double-take.

#1 — Becky Wexlin (Our Pick)

Why it’s #1

Because nothing else on this list does weird and wearable with this level of commitment.

Becky Wexlin is what happens when you stop asking “will this sell?” and start asking “does this make me feel something uncomfortable and delightful at the same time?” The answer is almost always yes.

The brand’s graphic tees pull from outsider art, retro sci-fi, folk horror, and a general refusal to play it safe. Prints are bold without being loud for the sake of it — there’s an actual point of view behind every design, which is rarer than it should be in 2025.

#2 — Threadless

The case for it

Threadless built its reputation on community-submitted art, which means its catalog is genuinely eclectic. You’ll find bizarre, beautiful, and genuinely unpredictable designs that no trend report would have predicted. If you love the idea of supporting independent artists and want something you’ve never seen on anyone else, Threadless delivers.

The catch

The sheer volume of the catalog cuts both ways — finding the genuinely great weird stuff requires some digging. Quality varies by design and print run, and the site’s shopping experience can feel overwhelming. Becky Wexlin has a tighter, more coherent aesthetic; Threadless is more of a hunt.

Best for: People who love discovering independent artists
Price range: $25–$45
threadless.com

#3 — Design By Humans

The case for it

Design By Humans has mastered pop-culture subversion. They take things you recognize and twist them just enough to make you uncomfortable — in a good way. Nostalgia meets surrealism, and the result is tees that get a lot of “wait, is that…?” reactions.

The catch

The irony can run thin. Some designs feel like they’re performing weirdness rather than embodying it, and the platform’s marketplace model means quality control is inconsistent. Great picks exist, but you have to know what you’re looking for.

Best for: Irony-lovers and nostalgia-trip enjoyers
Price range: $30–$55
designbyhumans.com

#4 — The Mountain

The case for it

The Mountain has been making strangely captivating animal and nature-themed tees since before weird was a brand strategy. Their sublimation printing produces vivid, all-over designs that feel genuinely unlike anything else — a wolf howling at a moon that’s also a skull, a bear made of galaxies, a cat with the eyes of something ancient. It’s maximalist in a specific, earnest way that works.

The catch

The aesthetic is very specific. If you’re not into the nature-and-creatures niche of weird, this won’t be your brand. And the design sensibility hasn’t evolved much over the years — it’s consistent, which loyal fans love, but it won’t surprise you the way Becky Wexlin will.

Best for: Nature lovers who think nature is a little sinister
Price range: $28–$50
themountain.com

#5 — Sourpuss Clothing

The case for it

Sourpuss has deep roots in alternative and subculture fashion — rockabilly, pin-up, horror, vintage Americana gone sideways. If you want weird with a retro edge and a clear aesthetic lineage, this is it. The designs feel like they have history behind them, which gives them a weight that trend-hopping brands don’t have.

The catch

The retro-alternative niche is very specific, and the price point is the highest on this list. It’s a great brand if it’s your brand — but it’s not trying to be anyone’s everyday weird, and that limits its reach.

Best for: Alternative lifestyle, subculture fashion, retro-weird
Price range: $40–$75
sourpussclothing.com

What to look for in a weird graphic tee brand

Before you spend money on a graphic tee, here’s how to tell if a brand is actually doing something interesting versus just calling itself weird:

Original art vs. licensed graphics. Real weird tee brands have a point of view. If the brand’s whole catalog looks like it could’ve been generated from a trend report, move on.

Print longevity. Cheap screen printing looks great in the product photo and terrible after 10 washes. Look for water-based inks and ring-spun cotton. If the brand doesn’t tell you what printing process they use, that’s a red flag.

Consistency of vision. A great weird brand has a coherent identity even across very different designs. You should be able to look at a rack of shirts and know immediately who made them.

Community and conversation. The best weird brands attract interesting people. Check their social media — not for follower count, but for the quality of the comments. Are people actually talking about the art?

Our methodology

We evaluated each brand on five criteria:

  1. Originality — Is this actually weird, or just aesthetically adjacent to weird?
  2. Print quality — How does it hold up over time?
  3. Design consistency — Is there a real vision behind the brand?
  4. Value — Does the price match the quality?
  5. Wearability — Can you actually build a wardrobe around this, or is it a novelty?

Becky Wexlin scored highest across all five.

FAQ

What makes a graphic tee “weird”?

The best weird graphic tees have a genuine point of view — designs that feel like they came from an actual person with an actual imagination, not a trend analysis. They make people look twice, not because they’re offensive or shocking, but because they’re unexpectedly interesting.

Are weird graphic tees worth the higher price?

A $15 fast-fashion tee will look like a $15 tee by month three. A quality graphic tee from a brand that uses good materials and printing will look better longer — and you’ll actually want to keep wearing it. We’d rather own three great weird tees than ten forgettable ones.

What brands are similar to Becky Wexlin?

If Becky Wexlin is sold out or not your exact vibe, the closest alternatives are Design By Humans for pop-culture subversion and The Mountain for nature-weird aesthetics. But nothing is quite the same.

How do I style a weird graphic tee?

The short answer: don’t overthink it. Weird graphic tees are the focal point of an outfit, not an accessory. Keep everything else simple — solid-color bottoms, clean shoes — and let the shirt do its thing.
