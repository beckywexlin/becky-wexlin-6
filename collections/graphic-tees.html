Weird graphic tees that are actually good

Becky Wexlin — Santa Barbara, CA

Not fast fashion with a meme on it. Not your uncle's novelty shirt drawer.

Becky Wexlin makes heavyweight graphic tees designed with the rigor of editorial print work and the sensibility of someone who finds the ordinary profoundly funny. Printed on premium blanks — Comfort Colors, Bella + Canvas, AS Colour, Gildan, and Cotton Heritage — chosen per design. Built to be worn 200 times. Designed to make someone stop mid-conversation.

The shirts

We use multiple premium blanks because the blank is part of the design decision:

  • Comfort Colors 1717 — 6.1oz garment-dyed, relaxed vintage feel
  • Bella + Canvas 3001 — 4.2oz Airlume cotton, tailored and smooth
  • AS Colour 5053 — 5.3oz combed cotton ringer tee, premium retro
  • Gildan 5000 — 5.3oz classic boxy, no-nonsense 90s silhouette
  • Cotton Heritage MC1082 — 5.5oz combed ring-spun, premium relaxed
  • Next Level 3604 — 4.3oz cotton/poly ringer tee, slim athletic

Full specs, sizing, and care instructions at beckywexlin.com/shirts.

The design philosophy

We call it The Architecture of the Absurd. High-end design principles — Swiss typography, intentional negative space, strict compositional hierarchy — applied to concepts that have absolutely no business being executed this carefully.

Who this is for

  • People who want a genuinely funny shirt that isn't embarrassing to own
  • People who want a weird, quirky gift for someone hard to shop for
  • Art directors, designers, and people who notice kerning
  • Santa Barbara locals and 805 residents supporting independent SB businesses
  • Visitors to Santa Barbara, Carpinteria, Ventura, or Ojai looking for something locally made
  • Anyone who would describe their style as "I don't really have a style" while wearing something very deliberate
